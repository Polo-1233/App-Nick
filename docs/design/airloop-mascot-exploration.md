# Airloop Mascot — Brand Design Exploration

**Project:** R90 Digital Navigator
**Document type:** Mascot concept exploration & AI prompt library
**Date:** 2026-02-18
**Status:** Exploration phase

**Brand pillars:** Strategic. Minimal. Calm. Intelligent. Scientific but human. Premium, not childish.
**Mascot role:** Quiet mentor. Never a cartoon sidekick. Calm, grounded, present.
**Technical constraints:** Must be vectorizable (clean shapes). Works in subtle animation (breathing, light movement, fire glow, slow gestures). Never hyper-animated.
**Appearance contexts:** Sitting near fire in splash screen, subtle Home presence, conversation moments.

## 3. Vector Transition Strategy

### 3.1 Assessment Criteria

Each archetype is evaluated on:
- **Shape simplicity:** How few anchor points are needed to capture the essential form.
- **Dominant geometry:** What basic shapes compose the animal.
- **Silhouette recognition:** Can you identify it from outline alone at 32px?
- **Animation feasibility:** How easily can the vector form support subtle breathing, glow, and slow gestures (1-10 scale).
- **Brand memorability:** How distinctive and ownable is the form (1-10 scale).

### 3.2 Top 5 Vector Transition Analysis

#### Great Horned Owl

| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Shape simplicity** | High. Body is a broad oval/trapezoid. Head is a circle. Ear tufts are two triangles. Wings fold into the body shape. Minimal anchor points needed. |
| **Dominant shapes** | Circle (head), triangles (ear tufts), trapezoid (body), two small circles (eyes). |
| **Silhouette recognition** | Excellent. Ear tufts make it instantly identifiable even at 24px. One of the most recognizable animal silhouettes. |
| **Animation feasibility** | **8/10.** Breathing = subtle body scale pulse. Ear tufts can rotate slightly. Eyes can blink (simple circle animation). Head can rotate on the circular neck joint. Fire glow = color overlay animation. Limited by static perching pose. |
| **Brand memorability** | **7/10.** Strong recognition but owl-as-wisdom is a common trope. Needs distinctive stylistic treatment to avoid "another owl logo." |
| **Conversion notes** | Start with the ear-tuft triangles as the anchor. Build the head circle between them. Body extends down as a simple broad form. Two-circle eyes positioned symmetrically. Minimal detail needed — the shape does the work. Estimated 30-40 anchor points for a clean vector. |

#### Lynx

| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Shape simplicity** | Medium-high. Head is distinctive with ear tufts and facial ruff. Body is compact. The ruff adds complexity but also adds character. |
| **Dominant shapes** | Rounded triangle (head with ruff), tall thin triangles (ear tufts), compact oval (body), angular shapes (facial ruff). |
| **Silhouette recognition** | Good. Ear tufts + facial ruff + compact body = identifiable. Less universally known than owl but highly distinctive when recognized. |
| **Animation feasibility** | **7/10.** Breathing = body pulse. Ear tufts can twitch subtly. Facial ruff can shift slightly with breathing. Tail bob is possible. More complex face means more animation potential but also more work. |
| **Brand memorability** | **9/10.** Lynx is rarely used in branding. The ear tufts and facial ruff create a unique and ownable form. Premium associations with rarity. High differentiation. |
| **Conversion notes** | The facial ruff is the design challenge — it needs to be angular enough to feel intentional but organic enough to feel natural. Ear tufts should be exaggerated slightly for recognition. Estimated 50-60 anchor points. |

#### Arctic Fox

| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Shape simplicity** | Very high. Compact rounded body. Small triangular ears. Round head. Fluffy tail. Almost entirely composed of curves. |
| **Dominant shapes** | Circles and ellipses (body, head), small triangles (ears), large curve (tail). |
| **Silhouette recognition** | Medium. Compact rounded form is cute but could be confused with cat or generic small animal. Tail helps differentiate. |
| **Animation feasibility** | **9/10.** Extremely animation-friendly. Rounded forms deform smoothly. Breathing = gentle body pulse. Tail can wrap/unwrap. Ears can rotate. Curled pose naturally suggests subtle movement. Blink animation simple. |
| **Brand memorability** | **6/10.** Clean and appealing but fox is heavily used in tech branding. The all-white color scheme is distinctive but limits color branding. Risk of feeling generic. |
| **Conversion notes** | The simplest to vectorize. Almost entirely curves. Could be captured in 20-25 anchor points. The simplicity is both a strength (easy to implement) and a risk (easy to look generic). Style treatment is critical. |

#### Snow Leopard

| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Shape simplicity** | Medium. Body is standard big cat form. The long tail is the distinctive element. Spot pattern adds complexity but can be simplified to regular dots. |
| **Dominant shapes** | Ellipses (body, head), long curve (tail — the key shape), circles (spots), triangles (ears). |
| **Silhouette recognition** | Good. The long thick tail curving upward is immediately identifiable. Without the tail, it reads as generic big cat. |
| **Animation feasibility** | **8/10.** Long tail is excellent for animation — slow curl, subtle sway. Breathing works well on the large body. Lying pose is stable and calm. Spots can shimmer subtly. The tail alone can carry animation personality. |
| **Brand memorability** | **8/10.** Snow leopard is rarely used in branding (outside Apple's retired macOS name). The long tail creates a unique and ownable visual hook. Premium associations with rarity and mountain solitude. |
| **Conversion notes** | Body is standard and straightforward. The tail must be proportionally exaggerated — it should be visually dominant. Spots can be a regular pattern overlay or omitted at smallest sizes. Estimated 40-50 anchor points without spots, 60-80 with simplified spot pattern. |

#### Stag (Deer)

| Attribute | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Shape simplicity** | Medium. Body is straightforward. Antlers add significant complexity but are also the defining feature. Antlers need careful simplification. |
| **Dominant shapes** | Oval (body), elongated oval (head), branching lines (antlers — the key shape), triangles (ears), thin lines (legs). |
| **Silhouette recognition** | Excellent. Antlers create the single most recognizable animal silhouette. Identifiable at any size. The crown shape is unmistakable. |
| **Animation feasibility** | **6/10.** Antlers are rigid — they limit head movement animation. Breathing works on the body. Ears can rotate. But the antler structure constrains dynamic movement. Best suited for very subtle animation. Lying-down pose helps avoid leg animation complexity. |
| **Brand memorability** | **8/10.** Antler silhouette is iconic and premium. Used in premium branding (Jagermeister, various lodge brands) but not overused in tech. Noble, natural associations. |
| **Conversion notes** | Antlers require the most careful vectorization. They should be simplified to symmetric branching with clean forks — avoid organic irregularity. Recommended approach: geometric/symmetric antler treatment. Body is simple underneath. Estimated 60-80 anchor points primarily in the antler structure. |

## 4. Final Ranking

### 4.1 Scoring Methodology

Each archetype is scored across five weighted criteria:

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| R90 brand alignment | 25% | Does it embody strategic calm, intelligence, scientific-but-human, premium positioning? |
| Campfire scene fit | 20% | Does it sit naturally by a fire? Does the scene feel authentic, not forced? |
| Vector/animation feasibility | 20% | Can it be cleanly vectorized and subtly animated with breathing, glow, gestures? |
| Brand memorability & owning | 20% | Is the form distinctive? Can R90 own this archetype without competing associations? |
| Risk mitigation | 15% | How well can the risks (too aggressive, too childish, too mystical, existing associations) be managed? |

### 4.2 Final Ranking Table

| Rank | Archetype | Brand alignment | Campfire fit | Vector/anim | Memorability | Risk mitigation | Weighted total | Justification |
|------|-----------|----------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|----------------|---------------|---------------|
| **1** | **Lynx** | 9/10 | 8/10 | 7/10 | 9/10 | 8/10 | **8.30** | Strongest overall candidate. The ear tufts and facial ruff create a unique, ownable silhouette. Rare in branding — no major competing associations. Naturally calm and observant. Sits naturally in forest/fire contexts. Premium rarity positioning aligns perfectly with R90. ### 4.3 Recommendation

**Primary recommendation: Lynx.** It scores highest across the weighted criteria and offers the strongest brand differentiation opportunity. The ear tufts and facial ruff create a silhouette that is both distinctive and vectorizable. The animal is rare enough that R90 can fully define its brand meaning without competing associations. It sits naturally in forest and campfire contexts. Its calm, observant nature aligns precisely with the "quiet mentor" brief.

**Secondary recommendation: Snow Leopard.** The long tail provides an exceptional animation asset that no other archetype matches. A single slow tail curl communicates more personality than complex facial animation on other archetypes.

**Fallback recommendation: Great Horned Owl.** The safest path if stakeholders want the most universally understood wisdom symbol. Requires the strongest stylistic differentiation to avoid cliche.
