The Concept

Why 90 Minutes?

The number 90 is at the heart of everything Nick teaches. A complete sleep cycle takes approximately 90 minutes. This playbook is designed to be read in exactly one sleep cycle – 90 minutes of focused reading that will transform how you think about rest forever.

The Promise

In 90 minutes, you will understand the 7 Key Sleep Performance Indicators (KSPIs) that elite athletes use to optimise their recovery. You'll get one actionable "Quick Win" per chapter that you can implement tonight. And you'll have a clear map of where to go next.

What This Book Is NOT

This is NOT a replacement for the full SLEEP book (Penguin Random House)

This is NOT a substitute for personalised coaching

This is NOT an exhaustive scientific treatise

This IS your map. Nick is your guide. Let's begin.

Book Structure Overview

Chapter Template

Each chapter follows the same structure for consistency and ease of reading:

1. Opening Hook (100-150 words)

A story, scenario, or provocative statement that immediately grabs attention. Often a "what if" or a common misconception.

Example for Chapter 3: "What if I told you that the 8-hour sleep rule is a myth invented by mattress companies?"

2. The KSPI Explained (800-1000 words)

Clear, jargon-free explanation of the Key Sleep Performance Indicator. What it is, why it matters, how it works. Use analogies. Keep it accessible.

3. The Elite Edge (300-400 words)

A brief story or example from Nick's work with elite athletes. This is the proof, the credibility, the "this actually works" moment. Keep it short – just enough to validate the principle.

Example: "When I first introduced 90-minute cycle thinking to British Cycling, the coaches were sceptical. Within three months..."

4. The Common Mistakes (200-300 words)

What most people get wrong about this KSPI. This validates the reader's past struggles ("it's not your fault") while creating the need for the solution.

5. Quick Win (150-200 words)

ONE specific action the reader can take tonight or tomorrow morning. Not a complete solution – just enough to create a small victory and build momentum.

Format:

6. Bridge to Next Chapter (50-100 words)

A one-sentence teaser that connects this KSPI to the next one, creating momentum to keep reading.

Example: "Now that you understand your inner clock, it's time to discover what type of sleeper you actually are..."

Detailed Chapter Outlines

INTRODUCTION: Why Everything You Know Is Wrong

~1,500 words | 7-8 minutes

Key Messages:

The sleep industry has been lying to you (8 hours, perfect mattress, etc.)

Nick's origin story – 1998, Sir Alex Ferguson, Manchester United (brief)

The R90 approach: recovery as a skill, not a luxury

What the reader will gain from this 90-minute investment

"10 years later, we're still having the same conversations – it's time to change that"

Ends With: "Let's start with the most fundamental truth about your body..."

CHAPTER 1: Your Inner Clock (Circadian Rhythm)

~2,000 words | 10 minutes

Key Messages:

You have a 24-hour internal clock that governs everything

Light is the master switch – especially morning light

Modern life has broken our connection to natural rhythms

Elite athletes don't fight their biology – they work with it

Elite Edge Story: Brief mention of how understanding circadian rhythms changed training schedules at Manchester United

Common Mistakes: Relying on alarm clocks, avoiding morning light, inconsistent sleep times

Quick Win: "Tomorrow morning, within 30 minutes of waking, get outside or stand by a window for 2-5 minutes. No sunglasses. Let the light hit your eyes. Do this for 7 days and notice the difference."

CHAPTER 2: Your Sleep Personality (Chronotype)

~2,000 words | 10 minutes

Key Messages:

You were born a morning person or an evening person – it's genetic

Fighting your chronotype is fighting your DNA

Chrono-Nutrition: when you eat matters as much as what you eat

Chrono-Exercise: there's an optimal time for YOU to train

Elite Edge Story: How identifying chronotypes at Manchester United changed who performed in morning vs. afternoon training

Common Mistakes: Forcing early mornings when you're an evening type, eating at wrong times for your body

Quick Win: "Answer this: Do you naturally wake up early even without an alarm, or do you come alive after 9pm? There's no wrong answer – just YOUR answer. This week, notice when you feel most alert and most tired."

CHAPTER 3: The Anchor (ARP & 90-Minute Cycles)

~2,500 words | 12 minutes

⭐ THIS IS THE CORE OF R90 – THE LONGEST CHAPTER ⭐

Key Messages:

The 8-hour myth: you don't need 8 hours, you need complete cycles

One sleep cycle = 90 minutes (light sleep → deep sleep → REM → back up)

ARP (Anchor Rise Point): the most important time in your day

Calculate backwards: if ARP is 6:30am, ideal sleep times are 11pm (5 cycles), 12:30am (4 cycles), etc.

Think in cycles per week, not hours per night

Elite Edge Story: How British Cycling used cycle counting instead of hour counting, and how this changed pre-race preparation

Common Mistakes: Obsessing over 8 hours, variable wake times, waking mid-cycle

Quick Win: "Pick your ARP – the time you need to wake up most days. Now count backwards in 90-minute blocks. That's your menu of ideal bedtimes. Tonight, try hitting one of those times exactly."

CHAPTER 4: Daytime Recovery (CRPs & MRMs)

~2,000 words | 10 minutes

Key Messages:

Recovery isn't just night-time – it's a 24-hour process

CRPs (Controlled Recovery Periods): strategic 15-20 minute naps

MRMs (Mental Recovery Moments): micro-pauses throughout the day

Optimal CRP windows based on your ARP (typically early-mid afternoon)

Why "powering through" is a performance killer

Elite Edge Story: How the midday recovery room changed Manchester United's double training sessions

Common Mistakes: Napping too long (entering deep sleep), napping too late, ignoring energy dips

Quick Win: "Set a timer for 20 minutes this afternoon. Close your eyes. You don't need to sleep – just rest. Notice how you feel afterwards compared to reaching for coffee."

CHAPTER 5: Your Sleep Sanctuary (Environment)

~2,000 words | 10 minutes

Key Messages:

Your bedroom should feel like a cave: cool, dark, quiet

Less technology, not more (sleep trackers often make things worse)

Nature cues: your ancient brain still needs signals of safety

Temperature: cooler is almost always better

The bed itself: position, orientation, purpose

Elite Edge Story: Designing 80+ player rooms at Manchester City's academy – the principles behind each decision

Common Mistakes: Too much tech, too warm, using bed for work/phones, blackout neglect

Quick Win: "Tonight, remove ONE piece of technology from your bedroom. Phone charging in another room is the most powerful single change most people can make."

CHAPTER 6: Bookending Your Sleep (Routines)

~2,000 words | 10 minutes

Key Messages:

The most important thing for good sleep is what you do FIRST after waking

Morning routine: light, movement, hydration – in that order

Evening routine: what you DON'T do matters more than what you do

The 90-minute wind-down: preparing your body for sleep

Why evening routines are personal (no one-size-fits-all)

Elite Edge Story: Pre-match and post-match routines with Premier League players – the non-negotiables

Common Mistakes: Screens before bed (obvious but ignored), eating too late, stimulating activities in the wind-down window

Quick Win: "Tomorrow morning, before you check your phone, step outside or to a window for natural light. Just 60 seconds. Phone can wait. This single habit change cascades through your entire day."

CHAPTER 7: Your Personal R90 (Bringing It Together)

~1,500 words | 7-8 minutes

Key Messages:

You now have the map – all 7 KSPIs

But the map is not the territory – YOUR life is unique

How to start: pick ONE KSPI and master it before adding another

Think in weeks, not days – recovery is cumulative

The difference between knowing and implementing

This chapter is shorter because it's a bridge to the Outro and the call-to-action.
