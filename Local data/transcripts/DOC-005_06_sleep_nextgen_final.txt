6. The Next Generation – The Future of Sleep and Sleeping
Gen-S : Included in this article is just a drop in overview of what is currently trending in the world of sleep. And I am thrilled to see that sleep(ing) is finally taking centre stage as one of the – if not THE – most important health pillars. 
Research continues to explore this pillar including The American Academy of Sleep Medicine, with a major educational event for sleep medicine clinicians in 2024, highlighting the importance of emerging technologies and innovative clinical practices. 
However, I would add a point of caution: When we jump from No Education and therefore awareness position, adopting a Taken For Granted random approach into a very active, NOISY sleep space, we need to first, reflect on developing more human centric behaviours in our every day life. 
Sleep(ing) is a very natural human process, your first, your number one health and performance pillar. Get it in play, and everything else you aspire to will be optimised. 
All your brain (The Sleep Boss) requires is for YOU to help it make it happen for you more often than not. 
Trending 
Smart Mattress: Awareness around smart mattresses was the highest among sleep-related trends in 2023, just be cautious of the claims being made, tech in place and what data you want from them and WHY.

Earthing or Grounding: Practices have seen an increase in popularity, with searches for “grounding” having increased by 26% and expected to rise further in 2024. The theory behind earthing or grounding is that our bodies are naturally designed to be in direct contact with the Earth's surface. Modern life, with shoes, houses, and insulated floors, has disconnected us from this natural connection. Grounding proponents believe this disconnection leads to various health issues, including poor sleep, inflammation, and stress. 

Weighted Blankets: Used and said to improve sleep due to their focus on the psychological benefits of the deep pressure stimulation the blankets provide.

Scandinavian Sleeping Method: Where couples share the same bed but have individual comforters to cater for different temperature preferences. 
Bamboo Pyjamas: Have been growing in popularity due to their moisture-wicking properties and perceived benefits for temperature regulation during sleep.

Blackout Blinds Curtains: With an increase in light pollution, blackout curtains are becoming an even more popular for intervention. Remember diminished light and dark develops sleep onset, you do however need a light tigger to wake naturally. Not easy to wake in the dark.

Morning Blue Light Exposure: Search interest in morning sunlight, daylight, blue light exposure increased by 36% over the past year. This includes technical products allowing for sufficient light under and over exposure also in countries with diminished light over longer periods of the year.
Noise: Noise other than white (e.g., pink, or brown) was a popular trend for relaxation and improving sleep quality.
Non-Sleep Deep Rest (NSDR): A technique designed to help us rest while remaining alert. It is similar to some mindfulness techniques such as Yoga Nidra, but focuses mainly on guided breathing techniques, thus making sure that while you get optimal rest, you do not accidentally drift off into deep sleep. 
The new CRP (controlled recovery period) part of the R90 Technique  
Podcasts: Sleep podcasts experienced increased awareness in 2023, with the trend continuing into this year. The topics are divided between podcasts about sleep techniques and actual guided sleep meditations and sleep stories. 
Tracking and Monitoring: New technologies and software for tracking and monitoring sleep patterns are expected to become more advanced and mainstream, adding interactivity to the tracking functionality. Think before you track it! Why What When…
Mouth Taping: Which promotes nasal breathing during sleep, has seen a 109% increase in interest. Some professional athletes have also been quite vocal about this. Nasal breathing has proven health benefits: Remember you can achieve this naturally by training your self to breathe through your nose.
Sleepy Mocktails: Have become a trendy way to relax before sleep. They make use of herbal infusions, minerals, and fruit infusions. 
Sleep Tech for Children: There is growing interest in sleep technology specifically designed for children, such as sleep tracking devices and sleep-promoting toys which are not based on smartphones or tablets. Parent Alert.
The future of sleep for me and the new generation of high performance athletes, could not be further away from “sleep when you are dead”  and or “sleep is a waste of valuable time”. 
It will continue to be redefined as the High Performance Technique playing a crucial part in how we achieve success and even survive.
