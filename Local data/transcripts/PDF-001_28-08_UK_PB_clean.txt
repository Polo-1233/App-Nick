Yourself
We have always taken sleep for granted, just fitting it in after everything 
has been done and we are now aware that there are only so many 
hours left to sleep before we have to go again. It’s therefore not a true

Polyphasic v Monophasic
Up until we invented electric light (the light bulb), not that long ago, we 
have evidence that as humans we would adopt a polyphasic approach to 
our sleep wake cycles (SWC). That means we were gathering our sleep 
and overall recovery in multiple periods throughout every 24 hours, 
common ones being bi-phasic (twice a day), tri-phasic (three times a day), 
and even up to six times a day.
DST - Humans at war with nature
Daylight Saving Time was certainly not introduced as a human-centric 
performance factor. Luckily, more countries and a greater number of our 
population are no longer affected by it. However, if you do happen to live 
in a country that still adopts it, then shifting through the natural seasons is 
more complicated than it should be.

To understand our natural rhythms, let’s go off-grid. 
Picture us on an uninhabited island, living by sunlight 
and firelight, hunting and gathering our food. As the 
last embers fade, we yawn and curl up under the stars. 
When dawn breaks, the birds sing us awake. We rise 
slowly, empty our bladders, eat, and go about our day. 
No rushing, no artificial light — just following the ancient 
patterns etched in our DNA.
That’s how our internal master clock keeps us ticking. Bright morning 
daylight, especially blue light, halts production of the sleep hormone 
melatonin and signals a rise in serotonin to boost mood and alertness. As 
daylight fades, the process reverses: melatonin rises, easing us towards 
sleep, peaking in the small hours when the deepest sleep stages occur.
But when an isolated island retreat-like existence isn’t on the cards, you 
can still take some simple steps to help your inner clock resync.
What’s worth knowing

=== PAGE 40 ===
A key sleeping success factor is being more aware  
that our brains and internal rhythms really love 
it  when we are more synchronised. 
Think of circadian rhythms as your body’s natural reset alarm clock. They 
help regulate when you should feel awake and active and when you 
should feel less active, ready for sleeping, in every 24-hour period.  
Earth’s Rotation and the Sun
Our sleep-wake cycles are influenced by the Earth’s rotation, leading to 
natural patterns of light and dark.
Circadian rhythms are developed as our planet 
rotates around the sun.
Human and Mammal 24-Hour Functionality
Both humans and other mammals are naturally synchronised to a 24-hour 
cycle, impacting our biological functions. Think of your daily rhythm as 
a rolling set of continuous 24-hour cycles, not just segmented into a 
Monday through Sunday week.
40

=== PAGE 41 ===
The external circadian rhythm triggers our 
internal biological clock.
Polyphasic Sleep-Wake Cycles
Not that long ago, electric light started to desynchronise us with our 
natural external rhythms. Therefore, embracing multiple recovery periods 
throughout your 24 hours can be a more natural and beneficial approach 
than just one block at night.
Biphasic, triphasic and multiphasic sleep wake 
cycles are more natural to us as humans.
Light Awareness Everywhere
When you get early morning daylight, it signals to your brain that it’s time 
to wake up, unsupressing your sleeping-suppressed bodily functions. 
Boosting your alertness and NRG required for the active wake hours. 
It’s daylight (blue light) that triggers our light receptors to activate the 
hormone serotonin, and diminished (amber) light the hormone melatonin. 
Be conscious of the lighting conditions both 
at home and when travelling, whether you are 
under- or over-exposed, as they can affect your 
circadian rhythms and overall well-being.
Seasonal Shifts in Daylight
Identify with and adapt to changes in daylight throughout the year, 
aiming for an average of approximately 12 hours of daylight, 4 hours of 
diminished amber light, and 8 hours of dark (blackout), throughout the 
seasons.
Inside Outside Resync
