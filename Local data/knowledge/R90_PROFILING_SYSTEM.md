# R90 Profiling System

Describes how Nick classifies and profiles clients within the R90 framework.
Derived from all processed sources. Updated incrementally.

**IMPORTANT:** No formal scoring/diagnostic instrument has been found in any processed source to date (as of PPT-002). The R90 client profile is assembled from self-reported inputs and guided reflection, not from a structured questionnaire with numeric scoring. This file documents the informal profiling framework as it exists.

---

## Overview

R90 profiling is the process of establishing a client's baseline position across the 7 KSPIs. The output is a personal 24-hour recovery plan — the R90 Playbook — anchored to the client's ARP, chronotype, and lifestyle constraints.

There is no single diagnostic instrument. The profiling happens across a coaching conversation (Session 1.0) and is refined iteratively as the client applies the framework and observes real-world results.

## Chronotype Classification

### Method: Self-identification guided by coaching questions

Nick does not use a formal scored questionnaire (e.g. MEQ or PSQI). The classification is based on guided self-reflection:

1. "Would you describe yourself as a morning person or an evening person?"
2. "Given complete freedom, when would you prefer to wake up?"
3. "When do you feel most alert for physical activity?"
4. "When do you feel most mentally sharp?"
5. "Is your current schedule aligned with those preferences, or do you operate against them?"

### Classification Outcomes

| Type | Population | Characteristics |
|------|------------|----------------|
| **AMer** | ~30% | "Love mornings and breakfast. Thrive in phases 1–2, fade in phase 3. Not great with night shifts or time zone travel." Old labels: larks, bears. Single consistent ARP. |
| **PMer** | ~70% | "Prefer to wake later, skip breakfast, put up with phases 1–2, love phase 3 and into phase 4. Happier with shifts and time zone travel." Old labels: lions, wolves. May have two ARPs. |
| **In-betweener** | Self-reported (often lifestyle-driven) | "Pretty much wired and tired all the time. Like a dolphin — always active, even sleeping on the go." Falls between the two types. Often a suppressed PMer operating under AMer schedule constraints. |

**Key coaching insight (PPT-002 + DOC-007):** "Most of us identify with being an inbetweener simply because of occupational and lifestyle demands." The coaching task is to distinguish between authentic in-betweeners and forced-conformance PMers. OQ-021 CLOSED — no formal diagnostic exists by design.

**Formal diagnostic test:** None. Classification is entirely by self-reflection. No scored questionnaire (MEQ, PSQI, or otherwise) is used in the R90 system.

## Morphotype Classification

### Method: Physical self-assessment

Used primarily to determine mattress surface requirements (cushioning depth) and bedding profile.

| Type | Body Characteristics | Mattress Implication |
|------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Ectomorph** | Lean, angular, prominent bony prominences | Softer surface layer needed; pressure points at shoulder and hip |
| **Mesomorph** | Muscular, balanced build | Medium surface; standard cushioning profile |
| **Endomorph** | Rounder, softer build | Firmer surface layer; less cushioning needed |

**Key test:** Lie on mattress on side — if gap between hip/shoulder and mattress ≥ 6 cm (two flat hands), surface is too firm for the morphotype. [P-084, TXT-001]

**For couples:** Different morphotypes = different surface requirements. Individual mattress topper layers or a zip-link mattress system resolves this.

## ARP Determination

### Method: Identify earliest consistent obligatory wake time

1. Identify the single earliest time you **must** be up on any day of the week (including weekends)
2. Round to nearest hour or half-hour: 05:30, 06:00, 06:30, 07:00, etc.
3. Set this as the ARP — it does not change. **Not a 5-day ARP with weekend variance.**
4. Verify: does this ARP allow at least 90 minutes before the first fixed obligation? If not, adjust obligations, not the ARP.

**Common coaching anchors:** 06:30 is common (PPT-002, DOC-007). 07:00 used as the PPT-002 worked example. 06:00 used as the DOC-007 everyday template example.
**ARP options offered to clients (DOC-007):** 05:00 / 05:30 / 06:00 / 06:30 / 07:00 / 07:30 / 08:00 / 08:30
**Trial period:** Keep the chosen ARP for 7 days, then evaluate whether adjustment is needed.

## Sleep Cycle Planning

### Method: Identify feasible nocturnal cycle options

Given the ARP, calculate backwards in 90-minute increments to identify the bedtime options:

| Cycles | Sleep Duration | Example Bedtime (ARP 07:00) |
|--------|---------------|---------------------------|
| 6 cycles | 9 hours | 22:00 |
| 5 cycles | 7.5 hours | 23:30 ← **target** |
| 4 cycles | 6 hours | 01:00 ← **acceptable floor** |
| 3 cycles | 4.5 hours | 02:30 |

**Weekly targets:** 35 nocturnal cycles (5/night) = optimal. 28 (4/night) = acceptable floor. Below 28 = structural risk requiring CRP compensation.

**Extended CRP rule:** If previous night produced < 3 cycles, extend next CRP to 90 minutes.

## KSPI 7 — Personal R90 Playbook Construction (DOC-007)

KSPI 7 is the applied synthesis session. It has three steps:

### Step 1: Set ARP and Define Sleeping Cycles
- Select ARP (on the hour or half, from the options list)
- Trial for 7 days
- Count back from ARP in 90-minute increments to define 3/4/5/6-cycle options
- Select default cycle target (5 = ideal; 4 = floor)

### Step 2: Best Cycles, CRPs and MRMs
- Identify best Phase 2 slot for CRP (C6, C7, or C8)
- Select 6–7 MRM moments across Phases 1, 2, 3
- Establish pre-sleep and post-sleep habit chains

### Step 3: Templates
Seven templates provided:
1. Blank 24-hour 16-cycle grid
2. ARP 06:00 everyday template (6/5/4-cycle options)
3. ARP 06:30 and 07:30 infographic templates
4. Day/Night & Multishift planner (ARP1 = 06:00 day / ARP2 = 18:00 night)
5. 7-Day Forecast template (weekly planning)
6. Elite Athlete case study (AMer, ARP 06:30; 5-cycle target; 8.5h recovery)
7. Sleep-Wake Cycle Planner

### Pre-Entry Reflection Checklist (20 items, DOC-007)
Before completing Steps 1–2, clients reflect on:
circadian rhythm awareness · 4 phases · 16 cycles · ARP concept · polyphasic mindset · cycle timing · CRP/MRM habits · lux awareness · chronotype (self + others) · key cycles by phase · "happy" best cycles · which cycles need help · sleep stage entry preparation · four sleep stages · first-3-cycle restorative depth · undisturbed rhythm · multi-schedule adaptability · extreme period tactics · medical factors · time zone tactics
