## Methodology Structure

**OQ-001** ~~What exactly constitutes "right quality" sleep in Nick's framework?~~ **RESOLVED — DOC-002**
- Quality = completing full 90-minute cycles, waking at the end of a cycle (light stage), not mid-cycle
- Quality is structural (cycle completion + timing), not subjective (how you feel)
- Status: CLOSED

**OQ-002** ~~What specific "structured interventions throughout the day" does Nick recommend?~~ **RESOLVED — DOC-006**
- Full structure: ARP → 16 × 90-min cycles in 4 phases → MRM every cycle (phases 1–3) → CRP in Phase 2 (cycles 6/7/8) → nocturnal sleep in Phase 4
- Four phases now defined (see OQ-024)
- Status: CLOSED

**OQ-003** ~~Does Nick rank the 3 health pillars (sleep, exercise, nutrition) in a strict order?~~ **RESOLVED — TXT-001**
- The book describes a **three-axis model**: sleep/recovery + diet + exercise. Nick frames recovery as "the third pillar of our approach to a healthy life" alongside diet and exercise — suggesting a parallel structure, not a strict linear ranking.
- The "1st Health Pillar" language is consistent with rhetorical emphasis — sleep comes *first* in the coaching conversation, but the three pillars are interdependent.
- Status: CLOSED

## Terminology Gaps

**OQ-009** ~~What is ARP?~~ **RESOLVED — DOC-003**
- ARP = **Anchor Reset Point** — the fixed, consistent daily wake-up and start time
- It is the master anchor from which all 16 daily 90-minute cycles are organised
- Prior inference of "Anchor Recovery Point" was incorrect — corrected by DOC-003 explicit definition
- Status: CLOSED

**OQ-010** ~~What is CRP (Controlled Recovery Period)?~~ **RESOLVED — AUD-003**
- CRP = Controlled Recovery Period. A deliberate 20–30 min rest/nap.
- Scheduled midday or in the early evening (4–7pm window).
- Counts as a recovery cycle toward the 35/week target.
- Status: CLOSED

**OQ-011** ~~What are the 7 KSRIs exactly?~~ **RESOLVED — AUD-001**
- KSRI = Key Sleep Recovery Indicator (Nick confirms in first sentence of Lesson 1)
- 7 KSRIs: 1. Circadian Rhythm, 2. Chronotype, 3. Recovery Cycles, 4. Recovery Rhythm, 5. Environment, 6. Product Interventions, 7. Redefining Behaviour
- Status: CLOSED

**OQ-012** ~~What are the "7 Sleep KPIs" referenced in `R90-T 7 sleep KPIs.png`?~~ **RESOLVED — IMG-002**

**OQ-016** What is "urgent need" exactly?
- AUD-003 does not use this term; the 90-minute cycle rhythm implies it (each cycle boundary = a recovery signal)
- Status: OPEN — likely in AUD-004 (Recovery Rhythm)

**OQ-021** ~~Is "in-betweener" formally defined? Is there a diagnostic tool?~~ **RESOLVED — DOC-007**
- DOC-007 (KSPI 2): In-betweener = "pretty much wired and tired all the time. Like a dolphin always active, even sleeping on the go." Falls between typical morning and evening types.
- No formal scoring diagnostic exists. Classification is entirely by self-reflection guided by coaching questions.
- PPT-002 clarifies many self-identified in-betweeners are actually lifestyle-suppressed PMers.
- Status: CLOSED — description confirmed; no formal instrument exists (by design, not by omission)

**OQ-022** ~~Is "6:30 AM" a Nick-standard coaching anchor or his personal example only?~~ **RESOLVED — PPT-002**
- PPT-002 slide 8: "Keep it simple to on the hour or half: **06:30 is common.**"
- Confirmed: 06:30 is presented as a common coaching anchor, not only Nick's personal example. The template deck also uses 07:00 as a worked example.
- The principle stands: ARP = earliest reliable wake time, on the hour or half-hour. 06:30 and 07:00 are both common coaching starting points.
- Status: CLOSED

**OQ-023** ~~What is the minimum viable cycles per night before intervention is required?~~ **RESOLVED — DOC-007**
- DOC-007 confirms: 4 cycles/night (6 hours) = acceptable floor; 28/week = "no-worries" minimum
- Weekly floor formula ("no-worries reality actuals"): 28 cycles + 35 MRMs + 5 CRPs
- Below 28 cycles/week: use CRP as primary compensation tool; never shift ARP
- No separate clinical threshold given beyond the 4-cycle floor — the system is designed to accommodate variation without further clinical triggers
- Status: CLOSED

**OQ-017** ~~What specific actions does Nick prescribe at the "point of wake"?~~ **RESOLVED — DOC-002**
- Post-sleep sequence: bladder → daylight → hydration → food → mental challenge → exercise → bowels
- Unhurried pace and sequence both matter
- Status: CLOSED
