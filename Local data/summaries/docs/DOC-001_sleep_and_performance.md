## 2. Main Ideas

1. Sleep is not a passive/waste state — it is the primary human recovery process
2. Sleep deprivation degrades nearly every performance dimension: mental and physical
3. Sleep should be recognised as the **1st Health Pillar**, not secondary to exercise or nutrition
4. Structured, consistent intervention throughout the day is required — not just "sleep more"
5. Nick has 20+ years experience working with elite athletes globally

---

## 3. R90 Principles Extracted

**P-001** [CONFIRMED]
Sleep is the foundational recovery process. Nick explicitly frames it as "the bedrock of world-class performance." Not a rest state — an active regeneration state.

**P-002** [CONFIRMED]
Sleep deprivation has measurable effects on: information processing, emotional response, mood, motivation, skill acquisition, decision-making, reaction time, awareness, alertness, stamina, and relationships.

**P-003** [CONFIRMED]
Sleep is the **1st Health Pillar** — positioned above or equal to nutrition and exercise in Nick's framework. Mental and physical health are described as interconnected through sleep.

**P-004** [CONFIRMED]
The goal is not just quantity ("right amount") but quality ("right quality"). Both are required.

**P-005** [CONFIRMED]
Nick uses the term **"personal best" (PB)** as the target state — the idea that everyone has a recoverable optimum level of performance, consistently achievable through proper sleep.

**P-006** [STRONG INFERENCE]
Sleep is linked to systemic health risks: obesity, heart disease, stroke, diabetes, social isolation, addictive behaviours. Nick presents this as emerging science (not yet settled).

## 4. Behavioural Patterns Extracted

**B-001** [CONFIRMED]
Poor sleep leads to counterproductive behavioural changes that can become addictive — described as "dominant in our every day." This frames bad sleep hygiene as a self-reinforcing trap.

**B-002** [CONFIRMED]
People continue functioning under sleep deprivation ("crack on with your day") without recognising they are operating as "a shadow of their usual self." Normalised underperformance.

**B-003** [CONFIRMED]
Some individuals hold the belief that sleep is a waste of time or "something to do when you have passed away." Nick explicitly states he does not coach this mindset into elite athletes.

---

## 5. Decision Rules Extracted

**DR-001** [CONFIRMED]
When optimising performance, prioritise sleep before or alongside exercise and nutrition — not after.

**DR-002** [CONFIRMED]
Do not treat sleep as binary (slept / didn't sleep). Focus on quality AND quantity through structured daily interventions.
