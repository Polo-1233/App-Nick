## 2. Main Ideas

1. Electronic Insomnia is a rising pattern among athletes — the "can't switch it off" mindset
2. Random interventions in isolation (mattress, app, supplement, tracker) are counterproductive
3. Slow Wave Sleep (SWS/deep sleep) releases growth hormone — critical for physical recovery
4. Glycogen replenishment and protein synthesis during sleep depend on deep and REM cycles
5. ARP (Anchor Reset Point) is formally named as the consistent wake-up start time
6. MRM (Micro Recovery Moments) = 5-minute mental breaks every 90 minutes, formally named
7. CRP confirmed again: 20–30 minutes, midday or late afternoon

## 3. R90 Principles Extracted

**P-041** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
**ARP = Anchor Reset Point** (not "Recovery Point"). Nick's term for the consistent, fixed daily wake-up and start time. It is the master anchor around which all 90-minute cycles are organised.
> "Define your most consistent wake-up, start to your day time (ARP Anchor Reset Point)"
*(Note: OQ-009 had inferred "Anchor Recovery Point" — corrected here.)*

**P-042** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
**MRM = Micro Recovery Moments.** Formally defined: 5-minute mental breaks taken every 90-minute cycle throughout the day. Distinct from CRP (which is 20–30 min). MRMs are the lightest layer of the polyphasic recovery architecture.

**P-043** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
**Electronic Insomnia** — a category of sleep disruption driven by screens, gaming, social media, and virtual communication. Creates a "can't switch it off" mindset. Identified as a rapidly growing problem in athletes over the past decade.

**P-044** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
**Random interventions in isolation are counterproductive.** Buying a new mattress, pillow, tracker, or sleep app without a structural framework can worsen sleep and promote more consistent insomnia. "Using random interventions in isolation can develop other counterproductive side effects, and even promote more consistent periods of insomnia."

**P-045** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
**Slow Wave Sleep (SWS) / deep sleep** is critical for physical recovery:
- Growth hormone is released during SWS
- Longer SWS periods correlate with better daytime wakefulness
- SWS deprivation → increased daytime sleepiness + reduced athletic performance

**P-046** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
**Physical recovery during sleep is biochemically specific:**
- Glycogen stores are replenished
- Protein synthesis repairs and strengthens stressed muscles
These processes are "highly dependent on sleep, particularly the anabolic phases achieved during deep and REM sleep cycles." Without sleep, training gains are negated.

**P-047** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
The **"four phases"** of the day are referenced as an organising structure alongside 16 × 90-min cycles. High-demand phases alternate with rest/recovery phases across the 24h. Exact definition of the four phases not given in this article — needs confirmation from Playbook or AUD sources.

**P-048** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
**Competition eve sleep disruption is near-universal in athletes.** Study cited:
- 66% slept worse than normal before competition
- 70% reported problems falling asleep
- 43% reported early morning waking
- 32% reported waking during the night
This validates the scope of the problem Nick's methodology addresses.

## 4. Behavioural Patterns Extracted

**BP-020** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
**Electronic Insomnia pattern:** Athletes (and others) in a "can't switch it off" state — screens, social, gaming extending wakefulness into the melatonin window. Classified as a distinct modern pathology, not ordinary insomnia.

**BP-021** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
**Intervention scatter:** Responding to poor sleep by trying isolated products or techniques (mattress, app, meditation, tracker) without a structural plan. Each intervention may have merit in isolation but without a framework, they underperform and can compound the problem.

**BP-022** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
**Stimulant reliance in athletes:** Rising use of caffeine, energy drinks, herbal boosters, supplements to compensate for poor recovery. This is explicitly called out as a growing and counterproductive pattern — consistent with what AUD-002 identified as "camouflaging."

## 5. Decision Rules Extracted

**DR-023** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
**Do not use interventions in isolation.** Any sleep tool (mattress, tracker, supplement, technique) must sit within a structural framework (the SWR / 16-cycle plan). Without the framework, isolated tools are noise.

**DR-024** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
**Set your ARP (Anchor Reset Point)** — the fixed consistent daily wake-up time. Build all 16 ninety-minute cycles of the day outward from this point. This is the structural foundation of the R90 daily plan.

**DR-025** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
**Plan demanding phases and recovery phases across all 16 daily cycles.** High demand and rest must alternate. The day is not just "work then sleep" — it is a structured 24h cycle with deliberate recovery woven throughout.

**DR-026** [CONFIRMED — DOC-003]
**Take MRMs (Micro Recovery Moments):** minimum 5 minutes every 90-minute cycle. Even this minimal break positively impacts sleep onset ease, sleep maintenance, and deep sleep access at night.
