## 2. Main Ideas

1. Sleep is a four-stage process — not binary on/off
2. What matters most is not what happens during sleep but how waking hours are managed
3. Worrying about sleep is its biggest disruptor
4. The body operates in natural 90-minute cycles — 16 per 24-hour day
5. The R90 technique: count backwards from wake time in 90-minute increments
6. Post-sleep routine (what you do immediately after waking) is critical to overall sleep quality
7. Daylight triggers serotonin → sets circadian rhythm → enables tiredness at the right time

## 3. R90 Principles Extracted

**P-018** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
Sleep has **4 main stages**, each with a distinct purpose:
1. **Light sleep** — transitional, still somewhat aware of surroundings
2. **Deeper sleep** — body slows down
3. **Deep sleep** — body repairs, builds muscle/bone, strengthens immune system
4. **REM sleep** — brain sorts memories, solves problems, boosts creativity

**P-019** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
The body operates in natural **90-minute sleep cycles**. The R90 technique is built entirely on this. A full 24-hour day contains **16 × 90-minute cycles** — some for sleep, some for waking activity. This is the core unit of the R90 system.

**P-020** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
Nick names the framework explicitly: **"the R90 technique."** It turns 90-minute cycle science "into a whole new way of thinking about your day." Both sleep and waking life are structured around this unit.

**P-021** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
To determine sleep timing: **count backwards from desired wake time in 90-minute increments**. This ensures you wake during a lighter sleep stage, resulting in feeling refreshed rather than groggy.

**P-022** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
The **post-sleep period** (immediately after waking) is a critical phase. Key activities to reach a "fully human active wake state" include:
- Emptying the bladder
- Moving from darkness to daylight
- Hydrating
- Allowing appetite to develop and fuelling up
- Mental challenges
- Exercise
- Emptying the bowels
The order and unhurried pace matter. This directly answers OQ-017 (point of wake).

**P-023** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
**Daylight exposure after waking** triggers serotonin release — the "unsuppress bodily functions and get active" hormone. It also resets the circadian rhythm and ensures the body gets tired at the right time in the evening. This is a critical link in the daily cycle.

**P-024** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
The biggest disruptor of sleep is **worrying about sleep**. Nick states this twice, explicitly. Anxiety about sleep quality becomes self-defeating.

**P-025** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
**Daytime brain load directly affects night sleep quality.** Nick: "Our brain is in control when we enter a sleep state, put it under pressure during your day and guess what, lower levels of sleep quality is what you get as a result." How you live the day is inseparable from how you recover at night.

**P-026** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
The concept of a **Sleep-Wake Routine (SWR)** is introduced. Without structure underpinning the SWR, the ability to adapt and maximise recovery degrades. SWR is the operational container for R90 practice.

## 4. Behavioural Patterns Extracted

**BP-011** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
Treating sleep as a binary on/off switch ("I slept / I didn't sleep") is a fundamental misconception that R90 corrects. Sleep is a structured process with stages and cycles.

**BP-012** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
Rushing through the post-sleep period (alarm → immediate activity, no daylight, no hydration) disrupts the circadian reset and degrades the rest of the day. The post-sleep period requires an unhurried, sequenced approach.

**BP-013** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
Worrying about poor sleep creates a feedback loop that worsens sleep. R90 redirects focus to controllable process variables rather than sleep outcome anxiety.

## 5. Decision Rules Extracted

**DR-011** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
Structure sleep timing by counting **backwards from your target wake time in 90-minute increments**. This is the primary scheduling rule of R90.

**DR-012** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
Prioritise an **unhurried post-sleep routine** immediately after waking: bladder → daylight → hydration → food → mental/physical challenge → bowels. Sequence matters; rushing any step degrades the circadian reset.

**DR-013** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
Get **daylight exposure as soon as possible after waking**. This is described as "more important than you might think" — it activates serotonin and resets the internal clock.

**DR-014** [CONFIRMED — DOC-002]
Do not over-focus on sleep outcome metrics or worry about quality. Focus instead on process: doing the right things in the day and post-sleep period. Outcome anxiety is itself the problem.
