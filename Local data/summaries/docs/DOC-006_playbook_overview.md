Explicitly defined (line 280, 446, 600–606):
- **Phase 1:** ARP → Midday (cycles 1–4) — activation, activity
- **Phase 2:** Midday → Early Evening (cycles 5–8) — CRP window, continued activity

## Key Structural Content

### The 16 Cycles Example (5-cycle standard day, ARP 06:30)

- Sleep onset: 23:00 (or 00:30 if late home — wait for next cycle)
- Sleeping cycles 12–14: 23:00–03:30 — deep/REM stages 3 & 4
- Sleeping cycles 15–16: 03:30–06:30 — lighter sleep, readiness to wake
- ARP: 06:30
- CRP: Phase 2 (cycles 6/7/8, midday area)
- MRMs: cycles 1–10 (phases 1, 2, 3)

### 15-Minute Rule

If sleep doesn't come within 15 minutes: get up, stay in chillout mode, wait for the next 90-minute cycle boundary, then try again. Do not lie anxious in bed.
- Source: line 680, expanded in line 854

### Sleeping Position

- Non-dominant side, foetal position (left for lefties, right for righties).
- "Your non-dominant side is less sensitive (joints and muscles) and your dominant side is free to protect you, your heart and your genitals... as you enter those deeper restorative sleep stages." (line 792)
- This is the position the brain naturally prefers — aligned with sleeping outdoors.

### Environment / Sleeping Space Principles

- Bedroom = sensory sanctuary modelled on natural outdoor sleeping.
- Elements: natural colours, plants, familiar images/smells, blackout, cool pillow.
- "Strip your room back to an empty shell... reflect on what you would leave out that isn't essential for you and your brain to go sleeping." (lines 772–774)
- The primary bedroom association = sleeping (not work, not entertainment).
- Layered mattress system: build up layers, replace more frequently, portable for travel.

### Intervention Principles

- Interventions should be deliberately selected with clear "why, what, for how long" criteria.
- Trackers valuable but can increase anxiety — balance intrusive vs non-intrusive data.
- Caffeine/alcohol: "either not at all, or using your rhythmic 16 cycles to maintain a balanced intake approach with no highs and lows."
- No isolated quick fixes: any intervention that isn't embedded in the 16-cycle framework will underperform.
