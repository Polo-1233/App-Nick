## Key Content

### Sleeping Position — Foetal, Non-Dominant Side (confirms DOC-006)

- Nick sleeps on his **left side** (non-dominant, he's right-handed) in foetal position since childhood.
- Reason 1 — **Biomechanical:** non-dominant side is less sensitive; less joint/muscle pressure.
- Reason 2 — **Security:** dominant side is free to protect during sleep — a subconscious signal to the brain that it is safe to enter deep sleep states.
- Quote: "I've got my right side, dominant side, free to protect myself, so that gives me some level of security." (line 36–38)

### Bed Side for Couples

- When sharing a bed, both partners should ideally be on the side where they can face outward in their non-dominant/foetal position.
- For two right-handers: one should be on the right side of the bed (facing headboard view) — when they turn left (away from partner), they're on their non-dominant side. The other has a compromise.
- "When we have enjoyed our spooning, security and sex, when we actually want to go to sleep, we will turn away from each other." (lines 43–44)
- The critical point for product selection: products must be profiled to each individual, not shared uniformly.

### Pillow Principle

- If the **mattress surface is right** (soft enough to release the body in foetal position), no pillow is needed — or a very minimal one.
- Pillow problems: raises neck vertebrae, blocks airways → mouth breathing → snoring, dry mouth, neck/back pain.
- Device use (looking down at phone) is compounding cervical pressure — relevant to pillow selection.
- Quote: "If it didn't block my airways off, then maybe I wouldn't snort or mouth-breathe or get dry mouth or get problems with my neck." (lines 91–92)

### Mattress Check (Practical Test)

A field diagnostic Nick coaches athletes to use:
1. Lie on the **floor** in foetal position on non-dominant side (no mattress).
2. Observe the gap between cheek and floor — this is the gap that needs filling.
3. Repeat on your **actual mattress** — remove all bedding first.
4. Check: if gap is > **hand's width**, the mattress surface is too firm and "asking you to use pillows."
5. Solution: add a soft layer on top of the mattress surface; trial a layer from elsewhere before buying new products.

### Mattress Layering

- Principle: build sleep surface in **layers** rather than one rigid unit.
- Benefits: customisable; layers replaceable; portable (key layer can travel with athlete).
- Nick coaches athletes to take a key personal layer to hotels, racing boats, extreme locations.

### Individual Duvets for Couples

- Nick sees this as a standard practice in some countries, rarely done in UK/US.
- Rationale: different temperature preferences between partners → competing for shared duvet disrupts both.
- "Why do we both sleep under the same duvet? I like it hot, you like it cool." (lines 67–68)
- Individual duvets = each person manages their own temperature.

### Nose Breathing

- Natural sleep state = **nasal breathing** — gentle in/out through nose.
- Mouth breathing is prevalent but disruptive to sleep quality and airway health.
- Training nose breathing is a practical intervention — "various devices and little applications you can do."
- Quote: "When you're wandering into a sleep state, going to the bottom of those stairs, you want to be naturally breathing in and out through your nose very gently, just like you did as a child." (lines 111–113)

### Air Quality / Hypoallergenic Bedding

- Air around sleeping environment contains pollutants, allergens, dust mites.
- **Hypoallergenic bedding** (often synthetic) is preferred — doesn't harbour allergens as natural materials do.
- Bed linen should be washed frequently and aired.
- "You want a clean, fresh, hypoallergenic environment." (line 119)

### Fresh Bed Linen — Familiarisation Trigger

- Fresh linen creates a **familiarisation/sleep onset trigger** — the cooler, fresher feel signals to the brain that this is a good sleep environment.
- The effect diminishes by night 2, is gone by night 3.
- Increasing linen change frequency is a direct lever for sleep quality.
- Quote: "If you just increase the amount of times you change your linen, you're improving your ability to go into those sleep stages." (lines 146–148)
